LifeWeb's Zero Extinction Campaign focuses attention on the extinction crisis and provides a platform for countries to profile their financial needs to prevent species extinctions (Aichi Target 12), and achieve related targets as a key accomplishment in our global effort to stem biodiversity loss.

Extinction is a natural process, but most species are now threatened with extinction due to human activities. "Zero Extinction" is an ambitious goal, but science suggests that at least for vertebrate species much of the work needed to stave off many of the most predictable species losses can be accomplished through the effective conservation of a few hundred additional sites globally. Many of the most threatened species naturally have very small global ranges, and thus these conservation efforts do not necessarily require the protection of huge new areas. Conservation of these sites would be a huge step toward the accomplishment of Target 12 globally.

Why focus on preventing extinction?

One-third of global species assessed for threat are in danger of extinction. This phenomenon is thought to represent the sixth major extinction event on Earth and it is a major threat to the persistence of Earth's biodiversity, which is crucial to the proper functioning of ecological systems. The parties to the CBD have agreed to an explicit objective -Target 12- to prevent the extinction of known threatened species in the 2011-2020 Strategic Plan for Biodiversity. While ambitious, this target is achievable by 2020 with concerted effort by donors, governments, and civil society. Fewer than 600 sites have been identified to date, and already over half of these sites receive formal protection.

Strategies to prevent extinctions

The Zero Extinction Campaign advances two main approaches to address extinction threat:
  1. Protection of unprotected sites; and,
  2. Enhanced protection of currently protected sites.
A major contribution to accomplishing Target 12 and an endorsed indicator from the Conference of Parties is the conservation of sites where Endangered or Critically Endangered species are found at a single remaining location. Essentially, these are the most irreplaceable assets for biodiversity conservation on the planet.

How to Participate

LifeWeb's Zero Extinction campaign helps to finance biodiversity conservation at sites where Endangered or Critically Endangered species are found at a single remaining location on Earth. We achieve this by facilitating partnerships between public and private donors, and between developing countries and countries with economies in transition requiring funding.

Countries requiring funds should visit: lifeweb.cbd.int/share for more information and to access tools and data that will assist them in the preparation of a LifeWeb Expression of Interest.

Donors wishing to support this campaign should contact: lifeweb@cbd.int.
